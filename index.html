Legalytic – Legal Analysis as a Service ======================================== Version: v1.0.0-alpha Updated: 2025-05-19 [ai@legalytic ~]$ ./manifest --read Manifesto --------- (Conceptualized and authored by Mahir Sağlamöz – Founder of ThinkPoint [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/mahirsaglamoz)) We believe the language of law should be understandable by design. Legalytic is not just an AI tool; it's the infrastructure for legal clarity. We don’t offer legal advice. We offer insight, transparency, and a semantic understanding of what you sign. We are building a future where: - Legal documents are not just readable, but explainable. - Risk and responsibility are measurable, not assumed. - AI serves legal literacy, not replaces legal expertise. --- Legalytic = LAaS ---------------- Legal Analysis as a Service (LAaS) is a new category. It defines a modular, ethical, and scalable way to process legal text using language models. Why LAaS? - Because legal understanding is fragmented. - Because legal systems were built to be interpreted, not parsed. - Because contracts are still written in ways most people can't understand. Legalytic powers multiple products and touchpoints: - Legafy → fast, simplified contract analysis for individuals - Verifo → UI-driven clause-by-clause explanations - External API integrations → for compliance tools, HR systems, DMS platforms Product Certification System: - Legance Reviewed → Basic AI review - Legance Compliant → Risk-scored + clause-explained - Legance Trusted → High-quality verified legal clarity Feedback Layer: - Users can mark unclear areas → model improves with real use feedback Core Layers: - Clause Intelligence → clause tagging and classification - Ambiguity Resolution → flagging of undefined or vague sections - RIS Scoring Engine → risk quantification on a 0–100 scale - Multilingual AI Stack → contract understanding across jurisdictions Legal Clarity as Infrastructure ------------------------------- Did you know? ------------- Legalytic’s outputs are readable by humans, parsable by machines, and auditable by ethics boards. Our goal is not just automation — it's alignment with legal comprehension. Legalytic doesn't replace legal professionals. It supports them by: - surfacing hidden obligations, - simplifying complex clauses, - translating legal risks into understandable terms, - enabling scalable review of large volumes.
